Global Nepal Chamber of Commerce (GNCC)

Governance

The Global Nepal Chamber of Commerce (GNCC) is committed to principles of transparency, institutional integrity, and responsible leadership. GNCC operates as an independent, non-political, and member-driven global chamber platform.

The governance framework outlined below represents a foundational structure designed to support the responsible growth of GNCC and to evolve in line with international best practices and member participation.

Governance Structure

GNCC’s governance framework is designed to balance strategic direction, operational oversight, and broad-based representation.

  • General Membership: The collective body of members forming the foundation of GNCC’s representative mandate.
  • Executive Committee: Responsible for strategic oversight, policy direction, and stewardship of the chamber’s objectives.
  • Advisory Council: A consultative body providing independent guidance, expertise, and international perspective.
  • Secretariat: Responsible for day-to-day administration, coordination, and implementation of approved programmes and activities.

Executive Committee

The Executive Committee provides leadership and oversight to GNCC and acts in the best interests of the membership and the chamber’s mission.

The Executive Committee exercises its responsibilities through defined decision-making processes, delegated authorities, and periodic review mechanisms, ensuring accountability and continuity during the chamber’s founding phase.

During the interim phase, Executive Committee members may be appointed to establish foundational systems and processes. Future composition and selection mechanisms may include elections or member nominations, as determined by GNCC’s evolving governance framework.

Advisory Council

The Advisory Council supports GNCC by offering non-binding advice, strategic insight, and subject-matter expertise drawn from business, academia, and international institutions.

Advisory Council members do not hold executive authority and serve strictly in an advisory capacity.

Principles of Governance

  • Independence: Operating independently of political parties and partisan interests.
  • Transparency: Conducting decision-making in a clear and accountable manner.
  • Inclusivity: Encouraging participation across sectors, geographies, and communities.
  • Integrity: Upholding ethical conduct and responsible leadership.
  • Accountability: Ensuring leadership and structures remain accountable to the membership.

Evolution of Governance

GNCC recognises that governance frameworks must evolve as the organisation grows. This structure may be reviewed and refined periodically to reflect organisational maturity, geographic expansion, and member feedback.
